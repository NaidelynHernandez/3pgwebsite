Voter suppression



A Brief History

1700s: Voting Rights were solely given to White, Male landowners.

1800s: The passing of the 15th Amendment ensured that people could not be denied the right to vote based on race; half a million black men were now eligible to vote. However, the Southern States placed polling taxes and literacy tests to discourage Black men from excersing their right to vote. In 1869, Jim Crow Laws were established, enforcing segregation in the South.



1920: Women were given the right to vote with the ratification of the 19th amendment. Black Women, however, still faced widespread discrimination-especially in the South.







1965: The Voting Rights Act passes Congress, helping tear down barriers that limited African Americans and other marginalized groups from voting. 1972: Voting Rights Act is expanded to protect members of language minority groups; Required Voting Materials to be transcribed in different languages and provide multilingual aid.
